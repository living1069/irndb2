Immunological gene information

Name Description Date accessed Cite
InnateDB InnateDB contains mouse genes that play role in immunological processes mined from literature. This collection of genes, together with the SepticShock genes (see below), are the core of the mouse immunological gene set. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. May 2016
SepticShock Contains mouse genes that play role in immunological processes. The list includes genes with potential relevance to macrophage response to microbial stimulation. May 2016
ImmPort The list of immunologically related genes in ImmPort is a collection of human genes, which was formed with the goal of retrieving all genes that have immune system-related functions. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. May 2016
Immunome database Immunology-related human genes were collected from research articles, textbooks and electronic information sources. The list contains genes that are directly involved in immunological processes. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. May 2016
Immunogenetic Related Information Source This gene list, of human immune genes (April 28, 2013), is derived from the IRIS online database of known defence genes (note that the IRIS database does not seem to be available anymore). All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. May 2016
MAPK/NFKB network This human gene list was derived from in house curation of the p38, ERK, JNK signalling pathways (Raymond Lo and Fiona Roche) and the NFKB transcriptional regulatory module (Jenn Gardy). It contains 761 genes (April 28, 2013); members of the TNF-alpha and IFN-alpha signalling pathway derived from Transpath data were also included. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. May 2016
