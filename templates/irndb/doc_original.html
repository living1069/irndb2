The database for immunologically relevant noncoding-RNA (IRNdb) was developed foremost to aid our research into microRNA-regulated and lncRNA-regulated processes during murine tuberculosis infection. However, microRNAs in particular influence a wide variety of biological processes and as such may influence many pathways of the immune system. Thus, we present IRNdb to the wider research community as a tool to harness information on microRN-, piRNA-, and lncRNA-regulation in immunological research.

IRNdb combines information about murine microRNA/piRNA/lncRNA=>target regulation with information about the targets themselves, e.g. relevance to immunological processes, pathways, Gene Ontology terms as well as a variety of immunologically driven experiments. This is done to develop an understanding of the influence of the different RNA classes on the immune system. In IRNdb we focus on target genes with known immunological relevance in mouse and/or human that we mined from several sources.

{% comment %}

In IRNdb, one can browse microRNAs / piRNAs / lncRNAs or particular targets of interest. A researcher is able to browse pathways of interest for targets that are regulated by RNAs. There is a lot of information available for target geness, e.g. pathway information, gene ontology term associations, expression in biological/immunological-relevant experiments. Finally, we include our data on murine time-course tuberculosis infection.

Immunological gene information

Name Description Date accessed Cite
InnateDB InnateDB contains mouse genes that play role in immunological processes mined from literature. This collection of genes, together with the SepticShock genes (see below), are the core of the mouse immunological gene set. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. Nov 2014
SepticShock Contains mouse genes that play role in immunological processes. The list includes genes with potential relevance to macrophage response to microbial stimulation. Nov 2014
ImmPort The list of immunologically related genes in ImmPort is a collection of human genes, which was formed with the goal of retrieving all genes that have immune system-related functions. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. Nov 2014
Immunome database Immunology-related human genes were collected from research articles, textbooks and electronic information sources. The list contains genes that are directly involved in immunological processes. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. Nov 2014
Immunogenetic Related Information Source This gene list, of human immune genes (April 28, 2013), is derived from the IRIS online database of known defence genes (note that the IRIS database does not seem to be available anymore). All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. Nov 2014
MAPK/NFKB network This human gene list was derived from in house curation of the p38, ERK, JNK signalling pathways (Raymond Lo and Fiona Roche) and the NFKB transcriptional regulatory module (Jenn Gardy). It contains 761 genes (April 28, 2013); members of the TNF-alpha and IFN-alpha signalling pathway derived from Transpath data were also included. All human genes were converted to mouse via NCBI Homologene. Nov 2014
